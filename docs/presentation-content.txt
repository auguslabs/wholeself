WHOLE SELF COUNSELING - COMPREHENSIVE WEBSITE CONTENT STRATEGY
================================================================

================================================================
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------

WholeSelf Counseling - Comprehensive Strategy

This strategy combines the core messaging from WholeSelf Counseling with industry best practices for mental health websites.

Priority approach:
- Crisis-ready credibility (prominent emergency resources)
- Logistics-driven UX clarity (insurance, pricing, FAQs upfront)
- Empathetic, authoritative content (condition-specific pages + human tone)
- Calm, readable UI (blue/green/neutrals with one CTA contrast color)
- Segmented, emotionally meaningful CTAs


INDUSTRY REQUIREMENTS
---------------------

Industry Standards

- 24/7 crisis accessibility (ethical baseline)
- Logistical clarity as core trust factor
- "What to Expect" section (reduces dropout)
- Simple but exhaustive UX architecture
- Condition-specific pages for SEO + emotional validation

UX Principles
- Reduce cognitive load and anxiety
- Remove logistical friction
- Prioritize clarity around insurance, payment, FAQs
- Unambiguous navigation
- Logistics-first architecture


================================================================
PAGE-BY-PAGE STRATEGY
================================================================

HOME PAGE - HERO SECTION
------------------------

Home Page

Main Headline:
"A safe space for your healing journey"

Hero/Welcome Section:
WholeSelf Counseling is built on the belief that everyone deserves a space where they feel seen, supported, and safe. Whether you're navigating trauma, identity, transition, or simply seeking clarity in your life, this is a place where your whole self is welcomed. Our approach centers marginalized communities, cultural and personal identities, and the unique needs of every person who walks through our doors—physically or virtually.

Key elements:
- Centered content with background image
- Full-width hero section
- Large, readable typography
- Image should be subtle, not overwhelming text


SEGMENTED CTAs
--------------

Segmented Call-to-Actions

Emotionally meaningful CTAs addressing three main lead types:

1. "I Need Help" Button
   - Action: Direct to scheduling/contact form
   - Color: Contrasting CTA color (orange or soft purple)
   - Placement: Prominent, above the fold
   - Supporting text: "Take the first step at your own pace"

2. "My Loved One Needs Help" Button
   - Action: Information page for family/friends
   - Supporting text: "Resources and guidance for supporting someone you care about"

3. "I Need to Make a Referral" Button
   - Action: Referral pathway/form
   - Supporting text: "For healthcare providers and professionals"


CRISIS RESOURCES
----------------

Crisis/Emergency Resources (REQUIRED)

This section must always be visible and accessible. Meets industry credibility baseline.

Content to include:
- Crisis hotline numbers (National Suicide Prevention Lifeline, Crisis Text Line, etc.)
- Emergency resources
- Community mental health resources
- 24/7 crisis accessibility information
- When to seek immediate help

Important: Must always be visible (header/footer link) and use clear, non-stigmatizing language.


SERVICES PAGE
-------------

Services Page

Headline:
"Therapy designed for your whole self—personalized, intentional, and culturally rooted."

Content Structure:
- Service Categories: Individual Therapy, Group Therapy, Couples/Relationship Therapy, Family Therapy
- Therapeutic Approaches: Decolonial perspectives, inclusive practices, trauma-informed care, culturally-responsive therapy
- Specializations: Trauma and PTSD, identity exploration and transition, marginalized communities, LGBTQ+ affirming care, BIPOC-centered therapy
- "What to Expect": Critical section for UX - reduces anxiety and dropout
- Condition-Specific Pages: Anxiety, ADHD, Depression, Bipolar, etc. (SEO + emotional validation)


TEAM PAGE
---------

Team Page

Headline:
"Humanizing the service from the very first contact."

For each team member include:
- Professional Photo: Warm, approachable, authentic, high quality
- Name and Credentials: Licensure (LCSW, LPC, LMFT), years of experience
- Specializations: Areas of focus, populations served, therapeutic approaches
- Personal Approach/Philosophy: Therapeutic style, lived experience, cultural awareness
- Education and Training: Degrees, relevant training
- Contact/Scheduling: Link to schedule with this therapist


CONTACT PAGE
------------

Contact Page

Headline:
"Reach out when you're ready — we're here to meet you where you are."

Segmented Contact Forms:
- Form 1: "I Need Help" - Name, preferred contact method, best time to contact, brief message, insurance information
- Form 2: "My Loved One Needs Help" - Your name, relationship, contact information, brief description
- Form 3: "I Need to Make a Referral" - Name and credentials, organization, client information, reason for referral

Direct Contact Information:
- Office location(s) with full addresses
- Phone number(s) with hours
- Email address
- Office hours


INVESTMENT PAGE
--------------

Investment Page (Logistics-First Priority)

Headline:
"Your well-being is worth investing in—we make our pricing clear, accessible, and honest."

Content Structure:
- Pricing Structure: Individual Therapy, Group Therapy, Couples/Family Therapy - standard rates, session length, frequency options
- Accepted Insurance Plans: Complete list, how to verify coverage, out-of-network benefits
- Payment Options: Insurance billing, self-pay options, sliding scale (if offered), payment plans
- Comprehensive FAQ: Insurance coverage, out-of-pocket costs, deductibles and copays, cancellation policies
- Transparency Statement: Commitment to financial clarity, no surprise fees

Note: This page should be easily accessible from main navigation (logistics-first UX).


WHAT TO EXPECT PAGE
-------------------

"What to Expect" Page (Critical for UX)

Purpose: Addresses uncertainty and anxiety directly (major source of dropout prevention)

Key Sections:
- Before Your First Session: How to schedule, what to bring, what to expect emotionally, how to prepare
- Your First Session: What happens, how long it lasts, what questions you might be asked, confidentiality discussion
- Ongoing Therapy Sessions: Typical session structure, frequency, duration, what to expect over time
- Confidentiality and Privacy: What is confidential, limits, HIPAA information
- Timeline Expectations: How long therapy might take, when to expect changes
- Virtual vs. In-Person Options: Differences, how to choose, technical requirements
- Insurance and Payment Process: How insurance works, billing process

Important: This page should be prominently placed in navigation and linked from multiple pages.

TONE & STRATEGY
================================================================

CONTENT TONE AND VOICE
----------------------

Tone and Voice Principles

1. Professional and Deeply Human
   - Clinical expertise without being clinical
   - Warmth without being unprofessional
   - Authority with humility

2. Empathetic and Validating
   - Acknowledge difficulty
   - Normalize experiences
   - Offer hope
   - Reduce shame and stigma

3. Clear and Accessible
   - Avoid jargon when possible
   - Explain when necessary
   - Use plain language
   - Be direct but gentle

4. Inclusive and Affirming
   - Honor all identities
   - Use inclusive language
   - Acknowledge marginalization
   - Create belonging

5. Grounding and Reassuring
   - Reduce anxiety
   - Provide clarity
   - Set expectations
   - Build confidence


CTA STRATEGY
------------

Call-to-Action Strategy

Segmented CTAs (Three Main Pathways):

1. "I Need Help"
   - Primary CTA
   - Direct to scheduling/contact
   - Emotionally motivating language
   - Prominent placement

2. "My Loved One Needs Help"
   - Secondary pathway
   - Information for family/friends
   - Supportive, non-judgmental
   - Clear resources

3. "I Need to Make a Referral"
   - Professional pathway
   - Streamlined process
   - Clear information needed
   - Efficient workflow

CTA Placement:
- Above the fold on key pages
- Multiple strategic locations
- Consistent but not overwhelming
- Mobile-optimized
- Clear visual hierarchy

CTA Language:
- Emotionally meaningful
- Action-oriented
- Reassuring
- Clear about what happens next
- Examples: "I Need Help" (not "Contact Us"), "Take the First Step" (not "Submit")
