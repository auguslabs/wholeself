WHOLE SELF COUNSELING - COMPREHENSIVE WEBSITE CONTENT STRATEGY
================================================================

Document Created: Based on provided core page messages and strategic insights
Purpose: Detailed content strategy, layout recommendations, and design specifications for Whole Self Counseling website

================================================================
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================

This strategy combines the core messaging from Whole Self Counseling with industry best practices for mental health websites. The approach prioritizes:

1. Crisis-ready credibility (prominent emergency resources)
2. Logistics-driven UX clarity (insurance, pricing, FAQs upfront)
3. Empathetic, authoritative content (condition-specific pages + human tone)
4. Calm, readable UI (blue/green/neutrals with one CTA contrast color)
5. Segmented, emotionally meaningful CTAs
6. Technology: WordPress + Elementor for intake automation

================================================================
STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK
================================================================

INDUSTRY REQUIREMENTS:
- 24/7 crisis accessibility (ethical baseline)
- Logistical clarity as core trust factor
- "What to Expect" section (reduces dropout)
- Simple but exhaustive UX architecture
- Condition-specific pages for SEO + emotional validation

UX PRINCIPLES:
- Reduce cognitive load and anxiety
- Remove logistical friction
- Prioritize clarity around insurance, payment, FAQs
- Unambiguous navigation
- Logistics-first architecture

CONTENT APPROACH:
- Professional and deeply human tone
- Detailed condition-specific pages (Anxiety, ADHD, Depression, Bipolar, etc.)
- Empathetic microcopy throughout
- Credibility content (E-A-T: Expertise, Authority, Trust)

VISUAL STRATEGY:
- Blue → Trust, calm, professionalism
- Green → Growth and wellbeing
- Neutrals → Clean readability
- One contrasting color (orange or soft purple) → CTA visibility ONLY
- Calming environment, avoid drama, support grounding

CONVERSION STRATEGY:
- Segmented CTAs addressing three main lead types:
  * "I Need Help"
  * "My Loved One Needs Help"
  * "I Need to Make a Referral"
- Emotionally motivating language, not generic prompts

1. HOME PAGE
----------------------------------------------------------------

HEADLINE: "A safe space for your healing journey"
(Also in Spanish: "Un espacio seguro para el viaje a tu sanación")

SECTION 1: HERO/WELCOME SECTION

Layout:
- Centered content with background image
- Full-width hero section
- Large, readable typography
- Image should be subtle, not overwhelming text

Content Structure:
1. "Bienvenida" (Welcome) - Optional bilingual heading
2. Main Headline (Large, prominent): "A safe space for your healing journey"
3. Supporting Text:
   "Whole Self Counseling is built on the belief that everyone deserves a space where they feel seen, supported, and safe. Whether you're navigating trauma, identity, transition, or simply seeking clarity in your life, this is a place where your whole self is welcomed. Our approach centers marginalized communities, cultural and personal identities, and the unique needs of every person who walks through our doors—physically or virtually. Explore a space created for healing, connection, and growth at your pace."

Background Image Recommendations:
- Calming, therapeutic imagery
- Nature scenes (peaceful, safe spaces)
- Abstract healing imagery
- Diverse representation
- Warm, inviting atmosphere
- Should convey safety, inclusivity, and peace

Design Notes:
- Large, readable typography (minimum 24px for body, 48px+ for headline)
- Soft, warm color palette from brand guidelines
- High contrast between text and background for readability
- Image overlay (subtle darkening) if needed for text legibility
- Emphasis on inclusivity and safety in visual language

SECTION 2: SEGMENTED CALL-TO-ACTION

Primary CTAs (Emotionally Meaningful, Segmented):

1. "I Need Help" Button
   - Action: Direct to scheduling/contact form

   - Placement: Prominent, above the fold
   - Supporting text: "Take the first step at your own pace"

2. "My Loved One Needs Help" Button
   - Action: Information page for family/friends
   - Color: Secondary CTA color or same contrasting color
   - Supporting text: "Resources and guidance for supporting someone you care about"

3. "I Need to Make a Referral" Button
   - Action: Referral pathway/form
   - Color: Secondary or tertiary option
   - Supporting text: "For healthcare providers and professionals"

Secondary CTAs:
- "Learn About Our Services" (link to Services page)
- "Meet Our Team" (link to Team page)
- "What to Expect" (link to What to Expect page - critical for reducing anxiety)

- Prominent, accessible CTA buttons
- Clear value proposition emphasizing safety and inclusivity
- Trust indicators nearby (credentials, years of experience, accreditations)
- Mobile-optimized for speed and accessibility
- Buttons should be large enough for easy clicking (minimum 44x44px touch target)

TYPOGRAPHY BEST PRACTICES:

Headings:
- Large, readable (minimum 24px for body, 48px+ for headlines)
- Clear hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3)
- Warm, approachable font choices
- Sufficient spacing between heading and body text
- Use font weights to create hierarchy (700 for H1, 600 for H2/H3)

Body Text:
- Minimum 16px for body text (18px recommended for better readability)
- Line height: 1.5-1.6 for optimal readability
- Comfortable reading width (max 75-80 characters per line)
- Adequate spacing between paragraphs (1.5-2em)
- Use font weight 400 for body text (avoid lighter weights)

Font Loading:
- Use font-display: swap for web fonts (prevents invisible text)
- Preload critical fonts (H1 font)
- Consider system font stack as fallback
- Limit to 2-3 font families maximum

Accessibility Considerations:
- Professional yet approachable fonts
- Good readability on all devices (mobile, tablet, desktop)
- Consider dyslexia-friendly options (Inter, Open Sans, Lato are all good)
- Sufficient font sizes (minimum 16px for body)
- High contrast between text and background
- Test with screen readers
